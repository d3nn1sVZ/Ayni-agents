# Pitch 1-min: Ayni

*Skill: startup-pitch | Generated: 2026-04-26*

Two versions — same wedge, different room.

---

## Version A — Formal (Spiral demo day, investor event)

~150 words. ~1:00 delivery.

> Ayni is a marketplace where AI agents pay tribes of human experts via Lightning Network. An accounting agent asks our API a Peruvian tax question, pays 100 sats, and in the same second that payment auto-splits across all five contributors who maintain the tribe.
>
> **Stripe physically cannot do this.** A 30-cent minimum fee makes splitting a sub-cent payment five ways negative-sum. Lightning makes it free.
>
> We built it on Bitcoin mainnet during the hackathon. Two tribes live: Peruvian tax law and Spanish data science. Public agent-skill manifest at slash-well-known so agents self-discover. Live at ayniw.com, MIT on GitHub.
>
> Four builders in Lima. The experts we're monetizing — Latam contadores, regional lawyers — are people we can call today.
>
> Ayni is Quechua for reciprocity. We've encoded it in software. **We're asking Spiral to back the only payment rail that can.**

**Word count: 152**

## Version B — Casual (networking, hallway, after-party)

~140 words. ~55 seconds. More conversational, invites a follow-up question.

> So you know how AI agents are everywhere now and everyone's asking *who actually gets paid when an agent answers a question*? Right now it's the model providers and Stripe takes a thirty-cent cut and that's it. The human expert whose knowledge made the answer correct — they get nothing.
>
> We built Ayni. An agent calls our API, pays a hundred sats over Lightning, and that payment auto-splits across five human contributors in the same second. **Stripe literally cannot do that — the fee is bigger than the payment.** Lightning can.
>
> Two tribes live on mainnet right now: Peruvian tax law, Spanish data science. ayniw.com.
>
> Four of us in Lima. Hackathon-built last weekend. **Want to see the dashboard? You'll watch a payment fan out in real time.**

**Word count: 138**

## Delivery notes (both versions)

- **The single most important sentence is "Stripe physically cannot do this."** Pause after it. Let the listener register the claim. If they're a payments person, they will fact-check it in their head and conclude you're right — that's the moment the pitch is won.
- **Closing line is the call to action.** Formal version asks for backing; casual version asks for a demo. Don't soften the ask.
- **If you only have 30 seconds:** "Ayni lets AI agents pay tribes of human experts via Lightning. One agent payment auto-splits across all the contributors in the same second. Stripe can't do this — the fees are bigger than the payment. We built it on Bitcoin mainnet during the hackathon. Live at ayniw.com." (54 words.)

## Red flags

- **Don't say "blockchain."** Say Lightning. Bitcoin. Specific. "Blockchain" makes payments people tune out.
- **Don't say "Web3."** Same reason.
- **Don't open with the etymology of *ayni*.** Open with what it does. The cultural angle is a closer, not a hook.
